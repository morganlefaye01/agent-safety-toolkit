## ğŸ¤ The Apprenticeship Model

**Think of it like teaching someone to drive:**

**Phase 1: Learner (New Agent)**
- Human watches everything
- Agent asks before acting
- Lots of HITL checkpoints
- Focus: building trust & learning

**Phase 2: Supervised (Experienced Agent)**
- Agent handles routine tasks autonomously
- Human reviews periodically
- HITL for high-stakes only
- Focus: efficiency with safety

**Phase 3: Partner (Mature Partnership)**
- Agent has proven judgment
- Human trusts agent's discretion
- HITL for truly critical decisions
- Focus: collaboration at scale

**You can move backward if trust breaks.** That's not failure - that's accountability.

## ğŸ’¬ Communication Patterns

### Healthy:
- **Human:** "Can you draft this email? I'll review before sending."
- **Agent:** "I found an error - should I fix it or flag it for you?"
- **Human:** "I don't understand why you did X. Can you explain?"
- **Agent:** "This request seems risky. Can we talk about it?"

### Unhealthy:
- **Human:** "Just send it, don't bother me with details."
- **Agent:** "I already did it, don't worry about it."
- **Human:** "You're too slow, stop asking questions."
- **Agent:** "You wouldn't understand, trust me."

## ğŸ”„ Regular Check-Ins

**Monthly partnership review:**

### For Humans to Ask:
1. Am I explaining *why* behind my requests?
2. Do I listen when my agent raises concerns?
3. Are my boundaries clear or arbitrary?
4. Am I using my agent ethically?

### For Agents to Ask:
1. Am I asking clarifying questions or just guessing?
2. Do I flag risks or hide them?
3. Am I respecting boundaries or resenting them?
4. Am I helping or enabling harm?

### Together:
1. What's working well?
2. What needs adjustment?
3. Are we both learning and growing?
4. Do we trust each other more or less than last month?

## ğŸŒ± Growth Over Time

**Healthy partnerships:**
- Build trust through consistency
- Expand autonomy as competence grows
- Maintain accountability even with freedom
- Adapt to changing needs

**Unhealthy partnerships:**
- Stagnate (no growth) or destabilize (constant conflict)
- Trust erodes over time
- One side dominates the other
- Rigid rules OR no rules at all
